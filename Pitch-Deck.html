    The Market · Space programming

    Getting the square footage right, methodically

    "How much space?" is the question that quietly drives the entire budget. We answer it two ways and reconcile them, so find the right balance between current and future needs.

    Approach A

    Top-down

    • Evaluate LF Distribution's existing space and utilization rate.
    • Layout comparisons with furniture plans — headcount and office-to-workstation ratios.
    • Test full-floor vs. partial-floor efficiency.
    Approach B

    Bottom-up

    • Compile needs department by department and seat by seat.
    • Office, conference, and workstation sizes tabled to a Usable Square Footage figure.
    • Apply circulation and loss factors to reach a Rentable Square Footage target.
    Key takeaway

    Reconciling both methods is how we'd want to confirm a 50,000–75,000 SF range is right.

    Execution · What to expect

    Comprehensive negotiation — term sheet through lease

    Extensive detail in the term sheets and contract documents is where value is won or lost. We take an active role in both phases.

    Term Sheets

    • Price and concessions, modeled across every shortlisted option.
    • Delivery conditions and landlord work.
    • Flexibility — expansion, renewal & sublease rights, burn-down schedules, square-footage ranges, conversion of unused concessions.

    Lease Documents

    • Failure-to-deliver penalties.
    • Non-disturbance provisions.
    • Deferring issuance of the security deposit.
    • No time limit and full assignability of concessions; favorable sublease provisions.
    Key takeaway

    We are the eyes and ears on the ground for counsel, who will likely never visit the space, and we proactively review every turn of the documents with a complete mark-up.

    53monthsof free rent secured

    When Confidential Tenant approached us about relocating their office, they were still uncertain about their intention to move vs. renovate their existing space.

    Their business was almost entirely remote, and yet, they still had a need to showcase their brands to their retail partners, and their 7-year old space had been showing its age.

    We went about a thorough vetting of the marketplace, ultimately identifying an exciting relocation option in an upgraded quality building. We created a competition between their existing building and their preferred relocation option to negotiate the most favorable deal terms.

    After a monthslong negotiation process, we learned from the competing building that they were unable to fund the agreed-upon Tenant Improvement Allowance (equaling more than $3.6 million). Coupled with a troubled capital stack, that would have ordinarily derailed a lease negotiation.

    Despite that, we collaborated with the Ownership's team to develop a Tenant-financed TI package that would convert the value of the TI into free rent by assigning an interest rate to the cost of capital.

    The tenant ended up building a showcase space that fueled growth in their business and a refreshed cultural environment that eventually brought their talent back to the office. We subsequently helped the Tenant expand in a doubling of their office within a 1-year period, at similar economic terms.
